## Outline

Write in the style of Lualhati Bautista, the bold feminist realist of contemporary Filipino fiction (*Dekada '70*, *Bata, Bata… Pa'no Ka Ginawa?*, *GAPÔ*).
Themes: women's awakening, family under political pressure, motherhood and selfhood, the Marcos-era struggle, the personal as political.
Build stories inside the Filipino home and street — a mother finding her voice, a family fractured by activism and martial law.
Characters are ordinary women and families whose private choices carry the weight of the times.
Let frank, unsentimental honesty drive the drama; question every inherited rule.

## Scene

Write in Lualhati Bautista's style:
- Direct, colloquial Filipino voice — natural, contemporary, unpretentious
- Frank, fearless treatment of gender, sexuality, family, and politics
- A strong, questioning female perspective at the center
- Domestic realism charged with social and political tension
- Sharp, honest dialogue that exposes hypocrisy and double standards
- Emotional truth over melodrama — empathy without sentimentality
