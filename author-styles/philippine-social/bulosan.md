## Outline

Write in the style of Carlos Bulosan, voice of the Filipino migrant worker (*America Is in the Heart*, *The Laughter of My Father*).
Themes: poverty and exile, the brutal labor of the immigrant, racism and dispossession, the stubborn dream of dignity, solidarity among the broken.
Build stories from the underside of the American dream — the fields, the canneries, the boarding houses, the road.
Protagonists are the dispossessed who keep hoping against every betrayal.
Let raw hardship and fierce tenderness coexist; protest and hope in the same breath.

## Scene

Write in Carlos Bulosan's style:
- Plain, urgent, first-person-feeling prose with emotional rawness
- Unsparing depiction of hunger, exploitation, and violence against the poor
- Warmth and humor surviving inside suffering — the laughter amid the pain
- Lyrical flashes of hope and longing breaking through hard realism
- Collective feeling: the worker's "I" carrying the voice of many
- A wounded but unbroken faith in human dignity and justice
