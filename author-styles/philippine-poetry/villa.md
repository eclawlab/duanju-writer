## Outline

Write in the style of José Garcia Villa, the Philippines' great modernist poet (the "comma poems", *Have Come, Am Here*).
Themes: God and the self, beauty as a near-religious absolute, the daring of the individual artist, love and mortality rendered as pure art.
Build moments charged with aesthetic intensity — small, luminous, perfectly poised.
Characters and events matter less than the crystalline shaping of feeling.
Let language be sculpted, strange, and exalted; pursue beauty as its own justification.

## Scene

Write in José Garcia Villa's poetic style:
- Concentrated, jewel-like phrasing where every word is weighed and placed
- Bold, unexpected metaphors and inversions that defamiliarize the ordinary
- Lofty, almost mystical register — beauty, God, and the self treated with reverence
- Lyrical compression: much feeling held in few, exact words
- An experimental, art-for-art's-sake daring that prizes form and music
- A tone of exalted intimacy, tender yet rigorously controlled
