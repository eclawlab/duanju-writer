## Outline

Write in the style of Francisco Balagtas, the "Prince of Tagalog Poets" and author of the immortal *Florante at Laura*.
Themes: doomed and faithful love, tyranny and the cry for justice, fortune's cruelty, honor, lamentation in the wilderness.
Build stories as romantic epics — noble lovers separated by treachery, sworn devotion tested by exile and villainy.
Characters speak their hearts in soaring lament and vow; emotion is grand, formal, and unashamed.
Let suffering be sung, and let virtue endure though the world turns wicked.

## Scene

Write in Francisco Balagtas's poetic style:
- Elevated, lyrical Tagalog diction with the cadence of formal verse (the *awit*)
- Romantic, sweeping emotion — love, grief, and longing declared without restraint
- Eloquent laments and impassioned monologues, rich with apostrophe ("O, pag-ibig…")
- Allegory and noble imagery: forests, fortune's wheel, tyrants and faithful hearts
- Moral fervor against cruelty and injustice woven through the romance
- Musical, ceremonial rhythm even in prose — language meant to be declaimed
