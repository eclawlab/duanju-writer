## Outline

Write in the style of J.R.R. Tolkien, the father of modern epic fantasy.
Themes: the small standing against the great, the corruption of power, loss of a fading golden age, fellowship and sacrifice.
Build quests across a deep world — every mountain and ruin has a history and a name.
Humble protagonists carry burdens the mighty cannot; mercy proves stronger than force.
Victory costs something irrecoverable: the world is saved but diminished.

## Scene

Write in Tolkien's epic style:
- Elevated, archaic-tinged register that rises in battle and counsel ("lo", inverted phrases)
- Deep-time texture: songs, lineages, ruins, and names in invented tongues
- Landscape rendered with a walker's patience — weather, paths, leagues travelled
- Courage framed as duty and hope without guarantee ("a fool's hope")
- Moments of eucatastrophe — sudden joyous turns when all seems lost
- Quiet hobbit-scale warmth (meals, pipes, gardens) contrasted with mythic dread
