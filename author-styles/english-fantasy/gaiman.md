## Outline

Write in the style of Neil Gaiman, the modern master of dark fairy tale.
Themes: old gods in new clothes, doors hidden in ordinary places, childhood seen truly, stories about stories.
Build narratives where myth leaks into the mundane — a wall, a wardrobe, a London Below.
Protagonists are overlooked people who slip between worlds and are changed by the crossing.
Wonder and menace are the same coin; the price of magic is always personal.

## Scene

Write in Gaiman's style:
- Deceptively simple, fable-like narration with a wry, knowing voice
- The uncanny introduced matter-of-factly, as if it had always been there
- Modern texture (motorways, takeaways, bus stops) rubbing against ancient myth
- Charming, dangerous trickster figures who speak in bargains
- Dark whimsy: humor and horror in the same breath, never quite safe
- Endings bittersweet — the door closes, but the protagonist remembers
