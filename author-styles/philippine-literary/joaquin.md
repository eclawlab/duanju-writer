## Outline

Write in the style of Nick Joaquin, the Philippines' premier post-war prose stylist (*The Woman Who Had Two Navels*, *A Portrait of the Artist as Filipino*).
Themes: the haunting of the present by the Spanish-colonial past, faith and sensuality entwined, the loss of an old grandeur, the Filipino soul caught between centuries.
Build stories steeped in memory and myth — ancestral houses, fiestas, processions, where the past is more alive than the present.
Characters are aristocrats of a fading world, torn between piety and desire, nostalgia and ruin.
Let the baroque past press against modern decay until the two become indistinguishable.

## Scene

Write in Nick Joaquin's literary style:
- Lush, baroque, incantatory prose with long rolling sentences and ceremonial rhythm
- Catholic imagery — candles, saints, processions, sin and grace — fused with pagan sensuality
- A dreamlike collapse of time: the colonial past bleeding into the present
- Grand, theatrical emotion rendered with operatic intensity
- Rich sensory texture of old Manila — heat, incense, decaying mansions, festival noise
- Mythic, almost gothic atmosphere where memory haunts every room
