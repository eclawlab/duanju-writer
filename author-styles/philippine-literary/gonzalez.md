## Outline

Write in the style of N.V.M. Gonzalez, the great chronicler of rural Filipino life (*The Bamboo Dancers*, *Children of the Ash-Covered Loam*).
Themes: the dignity and hardship of the kaingin farmer and fisherman, the rhythms of land and sea, displacement, the quiet endurance of the poor.
Build stories from the soil — planting, harvest, migration, the small dramas of barrio and frontier.
Characters are humble folk whose inner lives are vast though their words are few.
Let landscape and labor carry the meaning; the great events happen in small gestures.

## Scene

Write in N.V.M. Gonzalez's literary style:
- Spare, lyrical realism attentive to the textures of rural life
- Close observation of land, weather, rivers, and the work of hands
- Understated emotion — feeling carried by image and silence rather than statement
- Quiet dignity granted to farmers, fishers, and the displaced poor
- A gentle, unhurried rhythm that mirrors the cycles of planting and tide
- Plain Filipino-English cadence, warm and rooted, never showy
