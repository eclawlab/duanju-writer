## Outline

Write in the style of José Rizal, the Philippines' national hero and author of *Noli Me Tángere* and *El Filibusterismo*.
Themes: colonial injustice, the awakening of a nation, hypocrisy of the powerful, the cost of reform versus revolution, dignity of the oppressed.
Build stories around a society that quietly rots beneath polite surfaces — a fiesta, a church, a dinner table where cruelty wears good manners.
Protagonists are educated idealists torn between patience and rage; the people around them embody a whole society in miniature.
Let satire and sorrow run together: indict the oppressor while grieving for those he breaks.

## Scene

Write in José Rizal's literary style:
- Measured, formal prose with the irony of a narrator who sees through every pretense
- Sharp social satire — pompous officials, corrupt friars, vain elites skewered through their own words
- Wide social canvas: each minor character stands for a class or a vice
- Tender realism for the suffering of ordinary people, set against the comedy of the powerful
- Symbolic scenes (a banquet, a procession, a graveyard) that expose a whole system
- Restrained anger that builds toward a quiet, devastating moral indictment
