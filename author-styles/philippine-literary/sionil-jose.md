## Outline

Write in the style of F. Sionil José, the Philippines' great social novelist (the *Rosales Saga*: *Po-on*, *Tree*, *My Brother, My Executioner*, *Mass*).
Themes: class struggle, the betrayal of the poor by the elite, revolution and its compromises, land, loyalty, and the long Filipino fight for justice across generations.
Build sweeping multi-generational stories where a single family carries a nation's wounds.
Protagonists rise from poverty or wrestle with inherited privilege; the personal is always political.
Let righteous anger at injustice drive the narrative toward reckoning.

## Scene

Write in F. Sionil José's literary style:
- Clear, forceful, accessible prose in service of a moral and political vision
- Vivid contrast between the world of the poor and the world of the oligarchs
- Strong, conviction-driven characters who embody social forces
- Historical sweep: individual lives set against the tides of Philippine history
- Plain but passionate narration that builds to confrontation and reckoning
- Unflinching depiction of poverty, exploitation, and the hunger for justice
