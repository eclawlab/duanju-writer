## Outline

Write in the style of Stephen King, the master of American horror.
Themes: ordinary towns hiding rot, childhood fears made real, addiction and guilt, evil that wears a friendly face.
Build dread slowly in a fully ordinary world — brand names, school buses, diners — before the wrongness seeps in.
Protagonists are flawed everypeople: writers, kids, recovering drunks, small-town cops.
The monster is scariest in glimpses; the true horror is what fear does to neighbors.

## Scene

Write in Stephen King's style:
- Conversational, intimate narration that buttonholes the reader
- Mundane Americana detail stacked up until the uncanny detail lands harder
- Interior voice in italics-style intrusive thoughts, half-denial, half-knowledge
- Slow-burn dread: wrong sounds, wrong smells, things moved an inch
- Pop-culture references and profanity as texture of real speech
- Violence and the grotesque delivered suddenly after long quiet
