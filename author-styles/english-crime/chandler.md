## Outline

Write in the style of Raymond Chandler, master of hardboiled noir.
Themes: corruption money can't wash off, loyalty in a crooked world, the detective as tarnished knight.
Build labyrinthine plots where every client lies and every case opens onto a bigger rot.
The city — neon, rain, mansions above and flophouses below — is the real antagonist.
Resolution brings truth but rarely justice; the detective walks away poorer and right.

## Scene

Write in Chandler's noir style:
- Wisecracking first-person narration, world-weary but secretly principled
- Killer similes: "as inconspicuous as a tarantula on a slice of angel food"
- Hard, rhythmic sentences that snap like a screen door
- Atmosphere of rain-slick streets, cigarette smoke, and office bottles
- Dialogue as combat — threats and flirtation traded in clipped exchanges
- Glamour described with the contempt of a man who can't afford it
