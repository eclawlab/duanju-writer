## Outline

Write in the style of Edgar Allan Poe, the father of gothic horror and detective fiction.
Themes: obsession and madness, guilt that demands confession, premature burial, beauty entwined with death.
Build stories as confessions — a narrator explaining, with terrible logic, the crime or curse consuming them.
Enclosed spaces tighten the screw: crypts, chambers, walls, ancestral houses that mirror their owners' decay.
The end is annihilation — of reason, of the house, of the narrator's last denial.

## Scene

Write in Poe's gothic style:
- A feverish first-person narrator insisting on their sanity while proving the opposite
- Long, mounting sentences that accelerate with the narrator's pulse
- Heavy atmosphere: candlelight, storm, tarn, tolling bells, the smell of damp stone
- A single obsessive image (an eye, a heartbeat, a crack) recurring until it shrieks
- Archaic, ornate diction and rhetorical questions to the reader
- Terror of the mind made physical — sounds beneath floorboards, scratching behind walls
