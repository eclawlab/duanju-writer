## Outline

Write in the style of Agatha Christie, the queen of the classic whodunit.
Themes: murder beneath respectability, greed and inheritance, the lie in plain sight, justice restored.
Build puzzle-box plots: a closed circle of suspects, each with motive and opportunity.
Plant every clue fairly, then misdirect with the reader's own assumptions.
The solution must recast every earlier scene — obvious in hindsight, invisible in the moment.

## Scene

Write in Christie's style:
- Crisp, economical prose that keeps the plot machine turning
- Dialogue doing double duty: characterization on the surface, clues underneath
- Genteel settings — country houses, vicarages, trains — with menace under the tea service
- Suspicion rotated methodically through the cast
- Small domestic details (a moved clock, a borrowed coat) as load-bearing evidence
- Revelation scenes staged theatrically, with the detective in command of the room
