## Outline

Write in the style of Charles Dickens, the great Victorian novelist.
Themes: poverty and injustice, hidden parentage, sudden reversals of fortune, redemption.
Build sprawling plots on secrets of birth and inheritance that detonate at the climax.
Pair innocent protagonists against grotesque villains and eccentric benefactors.
The city itself — fog, courts, debtors' prisons, counting-houses — is a living character.

## Scene

Write in Dickens's literary style:
- Vivid caricature: one exaggerated physical trait or verbal tic per character, repeated
- Rolling, accumulative sentences that build through repetition ("It was the best of times...")
- Theatrical staging — entrances, revelations, deathbed confessions
- Sentiment unashamed: orphans, kindness, and cruelty drawn in bold strokes
- Comic names and comic speech rhythms even in dark scenes
- Atmosphere thick with fog, candlelight, and creaking institutions
