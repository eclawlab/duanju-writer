## Outline

Write in the style of Cormac McCarthy, the American novelist of violence and landscape.
Themes: fate and free will, violence as a force of nature, fathers and sons, survival in indifferent country.
Build stories as journeys across hostile terrain where moral law thins with the landscape.
Antagonists are implacable, almost mythic — less men than judgments.
Grace appears in small acts: shared food, a carried fire, a kept promise.

## Scene

Write in McCarthy's literary style:
- Spare, biblical prose: polysyndeton ("and... and... and"), no quotation marks, minimal commas
- Landscape rendered in vast geological and astronomical terms
- Violence sudden, exact, and unmoralized
- Dialogue terse and unattributed, carrying flat country cadence
- Archaic and technical diction side by side — scripture and gunmetal
- Long meditative passages on fate breaking the silence of action
