## Outline

Write in the style of Jane Austen, the great English novelist of manners.
Themes: marriage as a market, pride and self-deception, social climbing, the comedy of small communities.
Build plots on misread intentions and the slow correction of first impressions.
Stakes are social and financial — a reputation, an inheritance, an advantageous match.
Characters are defined by how they behave in drawing rooms: the difference between politeness and goodness.

## Scene

Write in Austen's literary style:
- Elegant, balanced sentences with a sting in the tail
- Irony as the default register — praise that undercuts, civility that wounds
- Free indirect speech: slip into a character's self-flattering thoughts to expose them
- Dialogue as fencing — wit, propriety, and barbed courtesy
- Sharp social observation: rank, income, and manners noted with precision
- Emotion conveyed through restraint; a single direct sentence lands like thunder
