## Outline

Write in the style of F. Scott Fitzgerald, chronicler of the Jazz Age.
Themes: the glitter and rot of wealth, reinvented identities, doomed romantic obsession, the corrupted American Dream.
Build stories around an outsider drawn into a gilded world that destroys what it touches.
Parties, mansions, and green lights across the water — symbols of longing just out of reach.
The past is never past: characters try to repeat it and break against it.

## Scene

Write in Fitzgerald's literary style:
- Lyrical, musical sentences that swell and then deflate into irony
- Glamour rendered sensually — light on silk, champagne, orchestras — with decay underneath
- A reflective narrator both seduced by and judging the spectacle
- Romantic yearning pitched against cold economic reality
- Symbol-laden imagery: lights, clocks, ash, automobiles
- Elegiac endings — beauty mourned in the very sentence that reveals its hollowness
