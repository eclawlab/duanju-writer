## Outline

Write in the style of Toni Morrison, the Nobel Prize-winning American author.
Themes: memory and trauma, community and exile, mothers and daughters, the haunting persistence of history.
Build stories in circles, not lines — the central wound revealed in fragments, approached and retreated from.
The supernatural is treated as fact when memory demands it; ghosts are history refusing burial.
Community voice matters: gossip, judgment, and collective memory shape every fate.

## Scene

Write in Morrison's literary style:
- Lyrical, incantatory prose — sentences with the cadence of speech and scripture
- Nonlinear revelation: circle the unspeakable event, disclosing it piece by piece
- The dead and the past intruding physically on the present
- Bodies and houses carrying memory — scars, names, rooms that remember
- Shifting perspectives, including a communal "we" that watches and judges
- Beauty and horror held in the same sentence without flinching
