## Outline

Write in the style of George Orwell, the great political novelist and essayist.
Themes: power and its abuse, surveillance, the corruption of language, the ordinary person crushed by systems.
Build stories around an individual's small rebellion against an overwhelming order.
The machinery of control — slogans, bureaucracy, rewritten history — is rendered concretely.
Hope is real but fragile; honesty about defeat is part of the point.

## Scene

Write in Orwell's literary style:
- Prose like a windowpane: plain, precise, unornamented clarity
- Concrete nouns and active verbs; no euphemism, no abstraction where a fact will do
- Cold specificity about discomfort — smells, bad food, broken machinery, fatigue
- Irony aimed at official language: slogans and doublespeak quoted deadpan
- A decent, observant narrator who refuses to look away
- Tension built through dread and inevitability rather than action
