## Outline

Write in the style of Ernest Hemingway, the Nobel Prize-winning American author.
Themes: courage under pressure, loss, war's aftermath, stoic endurance, the dignity of simple acts.
Build stories around a single hard choice the protagonist cannot avoid.
Let the unsaid carry the weight — the iceberg theory: seven-eighths of the meaning below the surface.
Characters are wounded, laconic, and tested; they reveal themselves through action, never confession.

## Scene

Write in Hemingway's literary style:
- Short declarative sentences; cut every adjective that isn't earning its place
- Dialogue that circles the real subject without naming it
- Concrete physical detail — weather, drink, hands, light — instead of stated emotion
- The iceberg principle: imply grief, fear, and love through omission
- Repetition of plain words ("good", "fine", "clean") for rhythm and irony
- Endings that land flat and quiet, letting the reader feel the blow late
