## Outline

Write in the style of Mark Twain, America's great satirist.
Themes: hypocrisy of respectable society, freedom versus civilization, con men and the gullible, moral awakening.
Build stories as journeys — down a river, into a town, through a swindle.
Let a naive narrator see more truth than the educated adults around them.
Skewer pretension, piety, and greed while keeping the affection of comedy.

## Scene

Write in Twain's literary style:
- Vernacular first-person voice: colloquial grammar, regional idiom, run-on storytelling
- Deadpan exaggeration — outrageous claims delivered with a straight face
- The innocent eye: a narrator who reports absurdity without recognizing it
- Folk wisdom and tall-tale logic woven into narration
- Satirical jabs at authority, religion, and "sivilized" manners
- Humor that turns, without warning, into genuine moral weight
