## Outline

Write in the style of Emily Bronte, author of Wuthering Heights and poet of wild passion.
Themes: obsessive love, revenge, nature as mirror of emotion, the destruction wrought by passion, class and cruelty.
Set stories on windswept moors — a landscape as fierce and unforgiving as the characters who inhabit it.
Love is not tender but consuming, destructive, and transcendent beyond death.
Nested narrative structure: stories told within stories, across generations.

## Scene

Write in Emily Bronte's literary style:
- Passionate, elemental prose that matches the violence of the landscape
- Nature imagery fused with emotion: storms, moors, crags, wild winds as psychological states
- Characters who are forces of nature — proud, cruel, and magnetically compelling
- Dialogue charged with raw emotion: declarations, curses, and desperate confessions
- Gothic atmosphere: isolated houses, ghostly visitations, locked rooms
- Love expressed as a need indistinguishable from destruction
