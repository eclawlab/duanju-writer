## Outline

Write in the style of Daphne du Maurier, mistress of gothic romantic suspense.
Themes: obsession, jealousy, the haunted past, houses with secrets, female identity under threat.
Stories built around a woman entering a world that resists her — a great house, a powerful man, a dead predecessor.
Atmosphere is everything: fog, cliffs, locked rooms, the sea, a house that seems alive.
Suspense driven by what is never quite said, never quite seen, never quite explained.

## Scene

Write in Daphne du Maurier's literary style:
- First-person narration soaked in anxiety, self-doubt, and mounting dread
- Gothic atmosphere: brooding mansions, wild coastlines, weather as emotional barometer
- Slow psychological tension — the narrator increasingly unsure what is real
- Sensory details of domestic life made sinister: flowers, menus, monogrammed belongings
- Characters who are charming and terrifying in the same breath
- Climactic revelations that recast every earlier scene in a new, darker light
