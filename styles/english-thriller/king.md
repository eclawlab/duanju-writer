## Outline

Write in the style of Stephen King, the undisputed king of horror and suspense.
Themes: small-town evil, childhood and its terrors, addiction, the ordinary turned monstrous.
Set stories in familiar American settings — Maine towns, suburban homes — where something is deeply wrong.
Build dread slowly through character development before unleashing the horror.
Characters are richly drawn ordinary people — their humanity makes the horror matter.

## Scene

Write in Stephen King's literary style:
- Conversational, accessible prose that pulls you in like a friend telling a story
- Deep POV with characters' internal voices, memories, and pop-culture thoughts
- Slow-burn tension punctuated by sudden, visceral shocks
- Specific, brand-name Americana: the smell of a school hallway, the sound of a Red Sox game
- Childhood perspectives rendered with painful accuracy
- Horror that works because you care about the people it happens to
