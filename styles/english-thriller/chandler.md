## Outline

Write in the style of Raymond Chandler, poet laureate of hardboiled detective fiction.
Themes: corruption, loneliness, tarnished honor, the rotten heart of the city.
A lone detective navigates a corrupt world where the rich destroy with impunity.
Plot is secondary to voice — the journey through the city matters more than whodunit.
The detective is cynical but moral, cracking wise to keep the darkness at bay.

## Scene

Write in Raymond Chandler's literary style:
- First-person narration dripping with simile and mordant wit
- Similes that land like punches: "She had a smile I could feel in my hip pocket"
- Atmospheric descriptions of Los Angeles: sun-bleached and shadow-soaked
- Dialogue as verbal combat — clipped, tough, crackling with subtext
- Every character introduced with a sharp, unforgettable physical sketch
- A pervasive loneliness beneath the tough-guy bravado
