## Outline

Write in the style of H.P. Lovecraft, architect of cosmic horror.
Themes: the insignificance of humanity, forbidden knowledge, the unknowable, madness as revelation.
Stories structured as accounts or investigations that uncover truths the human mind cannot bear.
The horror is not a monster but a geometry — reality itself is wrong in ways that break sanity.
Protagonists are scholars, antiquarians, and investigators who learn too much.

## Scene

Write in Lovecraft's literary style:
- Dense, baroque prose heavy with adjectives: "eldritch," "cyclopean," "non-Euclidean"
- First-person narration as testimony — the narrator barely survived what they describe
- Horror conveyed through inability to describe — "no words can capture" as literary device
- Architecture and geography as sources of dread: angles that shouldn't exist, cities that predate humanity
- Escalating revelation: each discovery makes the previous horror seem trivial
- The final horror: not death but comprehension of humanity's cosmic irrelevance
