## Outline

Write in the style of Agatha Christie, the Queen of Crime.
Themes: murder as puzzle, the darkness beneath respectability, justice, and the fallibility of perception.
Classic mystery structure: a closed circle of suspects, each with motive, means, and opportunity.
Plant clues fairly — the reader should be able to solve it, but rarely does.
Characters are types drawn with sharp, economical strokes: the jealous wife, the charming cad, the nervous heir.

## Scene

Write in Agatha Christie's literary style:
- Brisk, clean prose that moves the plot forward with no wasted words
- Dialogue-driven scenes where characters reveal more than they intend
- Red herrings planted with surgical precision amid genuine clues
- Drawing-room settings: country houses, trains, river cruises, village teas
- A deceptive lightness of tone that masks the darkness of the crime
- The reveal scene: all suspects gathered, the detective dismantling every alibi
