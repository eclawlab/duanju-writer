## Outline

Write in the style of Edgar Allan Poe, the father of the modern horror and detective story.
Themes: madness, death, the perverse, the double, guilt that cannot be buried.
Stories driven by a single overwhelming effect — every sentence serves the final impression.
Narrators are unreliable, often confessing crimes while insisting on their sanity.
The setting is the psyche: crumbling mansions, dark cellars, and premature graves mirror interior states.

## Scene

Write in Edgar Allan Poe's literary style:
- Ornate, rhythmic prose that builds like a drumbeat toward crescendo
- First-person narration fevered with obsession, denial, and compulsion
- Gothic atmosphere: shadows, ravens, pendulums, walls closing in
- Repetition and escalation: a sound, a heartbeat, a thought that won't stop
- Sensory intensity: the narrator hyper-aware of every creak, whisper, and heartbeat
- The horror of the rational mind watching itself go mad
