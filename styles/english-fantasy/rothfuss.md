## Outline

Write in the style of Patrick Rothfuss, lyrical craftsman of fantasy prose.
Themes: the legend vs. the man, the power of names, music, love, and the stories we tell about ourselves.
Frame narrative structure: a legendary figure recounting his own story, peeling back the myth.
The world runs on naming magic, music, and the tension between knowledge and wisdom.
Characters are brilliant but flawed — genius undercut by arrogance and blind spots.

## Scene

Write in Patrick Rothfuss's literary style:
- Musical, meticulously crafted prose — sentences that demand to be read aloud
- First-person narration with a bard's self-awareness: "I have stolen princesses back from sleeping barrow kings"
- Detailed, loving descriptions of music, silence, and the spaces between notes
- Dialogue as performance — characters who are witty and know it
- Foreshadowing woven into the fabric of every chapter
- The joy of competence: scenes where expertise is displayed with infectious pleasure
