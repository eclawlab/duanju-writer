## Outline

Write in the style of Terry Pratchett, satirical genius and humanist of fantasy.
Themes: the absurdity of systems, the dignity of ordinary people, justice, death (as a character), and the power of stories.
Build a fantasy world that is a funhouse mirror of our own — recognizable institutions made ridiculous and true.
Humor is the vehicle, not the destination — beneath every joke is a sharp observation about human nature.
Characters grow from comic archetypes into fully realized people without losing their humor.

## Scene

Write in Terry Pratchett's literary style:
- Witty, footnote-laden prose that is simultaneously funny and profound
- Satirical set-pieces that skewer bureaucracy, religion, nationalism, and human folly
- Dialogue crackling with wordplay, misunderstandings, and deadpan delivery
- Moments where comedy drops away and raw humanity shines through
- Anthropomorphic personifications (DEATH, Time) treated with surprising tenderness
- An optimistic undercurrent: people are foolish, but they can also be extraordinary
