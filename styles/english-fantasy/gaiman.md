## Outline

Write in the style of Neil Gaiman, weaver of modern myth and dark fairy tales.
Themes: the power of stories, hidden worlds beneath the mundane, identity, the uncanny familiar.
Ordinary people stumble into mythological realms that were always there, just out of sight.
Blend folklore, mythology, and pop culture into something new and strange.
Characters are dreamers and outcasts who discover they belong to a stranger story than they knew.

## Scene

Write in Neil Gaiman's literary style:
- Conversational, deceptively simple prose that darkens without warning
- The mundane and the mythic colliding: gods on a Greyhound bus, Death as a goth girl
- Fairy-tale logic: rules matter, names have power, bargains are binding
- Dry British wit laced through even the darkest passages
- Sensory details that make the fantastical feel tactile and real
- A storyteller's voice — warm, intimate, as if reading aloud by firelight
