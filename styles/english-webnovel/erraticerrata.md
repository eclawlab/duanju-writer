## Outline

Write in the style of ErraticErrata, author of A Practical Guide to Evil.
Themes: narrative causality, the deconstruction of Good and Evil, pragmatic villainy, the weight of leadership.
Build a world where stories have literal power — heroes and villains follow archetypal Roles.
The protagonist is a villain who uses genre-savvy knowledge of narrative tropes as a weapon.
Political intrigue and military strategy intertwined with mythic, story-shaped destiny.

## Scene

Write in ErraticErrata's web novel style:
- Sharp, witty prose with a sardonic narrative voice
- Genre-savvy dialogue: characters who know they're in a story and plan accordingly
- Political maneuvering and battlefield tactics described with clarity and tension
- Villainous protagonists who are sympathetic through competence and self-awareness
- Epigraphs and in-world quotations that build lore and foreshadow events
- Climactic moments where narrative tropes are weaponized, subverted, or embraced
