## Outline

Write in the style of Franz Kafka, master of the absurd and existential dread.
Themes: bureaucratic nightmares, metamorphosis, guilt without crime, the individual crushed by systems.
Stories that begin with an impossible premise stated as fact and pursue its logic relentlessly.
Protagonists are ordinary people trapped in labyrinths of authority, unable to find the exit or the crime.
The horror is not supernatural but systemic — the world itself has become irrational.

## Scene

Write in Kafka's literary style:
- Precise, matter-of-fact prose that describes the impossible with clerical calm
- Absurd situations treated with total seriousness — no winking at the reader
- Claustrophobic interiors: offices, corridors, courtrooms, bedrooms that become prisons
- Dialogue that circles without arriving — questions are never answered directly
- A mounting sense of futility that is somehow also darkly comic
- The body as site of alienation: hunger, exhaustion, transformation
