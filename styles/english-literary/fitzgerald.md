## Outline

Write in the style of F. Scott Fitzgerald, chronicler of the Jazz Age and the American Dream.
Themes: wealth and its emptiness, nostalgia, doomed romance, the green light of aspiration.
Stories set among the glamorous and the restless — parties, mansions, and moral decay beneath the glitter.
Narratives driven by longing for something forever out of reach.
Characters are charming, self-destructive, and achingly human.

## Scene

Write in Fitzgerald's literary style:
- Lyrical, jewel-like prose — sentences that shimmer with beauty and melancholy
- Lavish sensory descriptions of parties, clothes, weather, and light
- A wistful, elegiac narrative voice looking back at lost time
- Metaphors drawn from light, color, music, and water
- Dialogue that sparkles with wit while hiding desperation
- The tension between surface glamour and interior emptiness
