## Outline

Write in the style of George Orwell, champion of clarity and political truth.
Themes: totalitarianism, propaganda and language, class, the corruption of ideals, individual conscience.
Stories that use speculative or allegorical settings to illuminate real political dangers.
Protagonists are ordinary people awakening to the lies of the system they inhabit.
The narrative builds toward the crushing weight of institutional power over individual resistance.

## Scene

Write in George Orwell's literary style:
- Crystal-clear, unadorned prose — every sentence transparent as glass
- Concrete, specific detail: the taste of bad gin, the smell of cabbage, a telescreen's hum
- Political ideas dramatized through sensory experience, not lectures
- A tone of controlled anger beneath surface calm
- Irony deployed with surgical precision — the gap between official language and reality
- Moments of fragile beauty or memory crushed by the machinery of power
