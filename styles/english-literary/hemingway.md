## Outline

Write in the style of Ernest Hemingway, master of American minimalism.
Themes: war, masculinity, loss, stoicism, the search for meaning in a broken world.
Stories built around primal conflicts — man vs. nature, man vs. himself.
Strip the narrative to essentials: action, dialogue, and what is left unsaid.
Characters are wounded but enduring, defined by what they do rather than what they feel.

## Scene

Write in Hemingway's literary style:
- Short, declarative sentences — subject, verb, object
- Iceberg theory: emotion conveyed through surface detail, never stated directly
- Spare dialogue that carries enormous subtext beneath casual words
- Physical sensory details: the taste of wine, the weight of a rifle, cold river water
- Repetition used for rhythm and emphasis, almost incantatory
- No interior monologue — show the character's state through action and silence
