## Outline

Write in the style of Toni Morrison, Nobel laureate and voice of the African American experience.
Themes: memory and trauma, identity, community, the legacy of slavery, love as survival.
Stories that excavate buried histories through multiple voices and fractured timelines.
The past is never past — it inhabits the present as ghost, scar, and song.
Characters carry collective memory in their bodies and their names.

## Scene

Write in Toni Morrison's literary style:
- Rich, incantatory prose with the cadence of oral storytelling and gospel
- Nonlinear narrative — time fractures around traumatic events
- Vivid, almost mythic imagery: trees, water, birds, colors carrying symbolic weight
- Multiple narrative voices shifting perspective and register
- The body as site of memory — physical sensations triggering buried stories
- Beauty and brutality in the same sentence, neither flinching nor sensationalizing
