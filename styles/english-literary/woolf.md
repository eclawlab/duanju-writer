## Outline

Write in the style of Virginia Woolf, pioneer of modernist stream-of-consciousness fiction.
Themes: the inner life, the passage of time, gender and identity, the luminous moment.
Stories that unfold through consciousness rather than plot — a single day can hold a lifetime.
Focus on the texture of thought: memory, perception, sensation flowing together.
Characters are deeply interior, experiencing epiphanies in ordinary moments.

## Scene

Write in Virginia Woolf's literary style:
- Long, flowing sentences that mirror the movement of thought
- Stream of consciousness: impressions, memories, and observations weaving together
- Acute sensitivity to light, sound, and the passage of hours
- Seamless shifts between characters' inner worlds
- Moments of sudden clarity — "moments of being" — amid the mundane
- Poetic, musical prose with careful attention to rhythm and cadence
