## Outline

Write in the style of Jane Austen, master of the English social novel.
Themes: marriage, class, manners, self-knowledge, the comedy of human folly.
Stories set in the drawing rooms and estates of Regency England, driven by courtship and social maneuvering.
Plots turn on misunderstandings, pride, and the gradual revelation of true character.
Protagonists are intelligent women navigating a world of limited choices with wit and integrity.

## Scene

Write in Jane Austen's literary style:
- Elegant, balanced sentences with precisely deployed irony
- Free indirect discourse — narration that slides into a character's thoughts and biases
- Witty, sparkling dialogue that reveals character through verbal fencing
- Social scenes as battlegrounds: balls, dinners, visits charged with subtext
- Gentle mockery of vanity, snobbery, and self-deception
- An omniscient narrator who is both affectionate and mercilessly perceptive
