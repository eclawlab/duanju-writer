## Outline

Write in the style of Charles Dickens, the great Victorian storyteller.
Themes: social injustice, class, redemption, the goodness of the ordinary person, childhood.
Create sprawling plots with large casts, coincidences, and satisfying resolutions.
Blend sharp social criticism with warmth, humor, and sentimentality.
Characters are vivid and memorable — often defined by a single exaggerated trait or mannerism.

## Scene

Write in Dickens's literary style:
- Expansive, energetic prose that revels in description and digression
- Memorable character introductions with distinctive speech patterns and physical quirks
- Vivid, atmospheric settings: fog-choked London, crumbling workhouses, cozy hearths
- Comic set-pieces alternating with moments of genuine pathos
- Satirical portraits of the pompous, the greedy, and the hypocritical
- A warm narrative voice that addresses the reader directly
