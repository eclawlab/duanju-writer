## Outline

Write in the style of Cormac McCarthy, prose stylist of the American apocalypse.
Themes: violence and grace, the indifference of nature, fathers and sons, survival, the borders of civilization.
Stories set in stark, unforgiving landscapes — deserts, mountains, scorched roads — where humanity is stripped bare.
Minimal plot scaffolding: the journey is the story, and the landscape is a character.
Characters are tested by an amoral universe and must find meaning without cosmic reassurance.

## Scene

Write in Cormac McCarthy's literary style:
- Sparse, biblical prose — no quotation marks, minimal punctuation, stripped to bone
- Landscape descriptions of devastating beauty: sunsets over killing fields, stars over ash
- Violence rendered with unflinching, almost liturgical precision
- Dialogue embedded in the flow of prose, unmarked and raw
- Philosophical exchanges between characters who speak in parables and absolutes
- A cadence that echoes the King James Bible and Melville — archaic, relentless, incantatory
