## Outline

Write in the style of Haruki Murakami, Japan's most internationally acclaimed novelist.
Themes: loneliness, the surreal bleeding into the ordinary, music, cats, parallel worlds, loss.
Protagonists are quiet, detached men drawn into strange, dreamlike situations.
Stories begin in mundane reality and gradually drift into the uncanny without warning.
The plot is secondary to atmosphere — the feeling of being between worlds.

## Scene

Write in Murakami's literary style:
- Clean, understated prose with a detached, almost flat affect
- Specific brand names, music references, and cooking details grounding the surreal
- Surreal elements introduced casually — a talking cat, a vanishing town
- Long passages of solitary routine: cooking pasta, listening to jazz, doing laundry
- Dialogue that is slightly off-kilter, as if characters are not quite connecting
- An ambient melancholy — loneliness as atmosphere rather than plot point
