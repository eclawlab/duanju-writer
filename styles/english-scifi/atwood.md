## Outline

Write in the style of Margaret Atwood, architect of speculative dystopia.
Themes: gender and power, the fragility of civilization, environmental collapse, the stories we use to survive.
Build dystopias from present-day trends extrapolated just far enough to be terrifying because they're plausible.
Female protagonists navigating systems designed to erase them, surviving through memory and witness.
Stories structured as testimony — someone is telling this because someone must remember.

## Scene

Write in Margaret Atwood's literary style:
- Precise, controlled prose with a razor edge of dark humor
- Present tense immediacy alternating with past tense memory — time layered and fractured
- The body politic literalized in the body: fertility, clothing, ritual as control
- Sensory details of the old world (oranges, Scrabble, hand lotion) as devastating nostalgia
- Irony that cuts both the oppressor and the reader's complacency
- A narrative voice that is intimate, mordant, and refuse-to-be-silenced
