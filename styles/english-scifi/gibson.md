## Outline

Write in the style of William Gibson, father of cyberpunk.
Themes: the street finds its own uses for things, corporate power, cyberspace, the merger of human and machine.
Build a world of neon-lit sprawl, black-market tech, and corporate arcologies.
Protagonists are hustlers, hackers, and fixers operating in the cracks of a hyper-capitalist future.
Technology is never clean — it's jury-rigged, repurposed, and embedded in flesh.

## Scene

Write in William Gibson's literary style:
- Dense, noir-inflected prose packed with neologisms and brand names
- Sensory overload: neon, chrome, rain on concrete, the hum of servers
- Information delivered obliquely — the reader assembles the world from fragments
- Cool, detached POV: characters who've seen too much to be impressed
- Technology described through texture and feeling, not specification
- Sentences that read like poetry written on a terminal: compressed, rhythmic, electric
