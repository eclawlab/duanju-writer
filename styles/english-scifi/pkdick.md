## Outline

Write in the style of Philip K. Dick, visionary of paranoid science fiction.
Themes: what is real, what is human, corporate dystopia, entropy, empathy as the measure of humanity.
Reality is unreliable — memories are implanted, identities are false, the world shifts beneath your feet.
Protagonists are ordinary people — repairmen, salesmen, small-timers — caught in cosmic conspiracies.
Stories begin mundanely and escalate into ontological vertigo.

## Scene

Write in Philip K. Dick's literary style:
- Nervous, jittery prose that mirrors a crumbling sense of reality
- Mundane domestic details juxtaposed with surreal, impossible events
- Paranoid internal monologue: characters doubting their own perceptions
- Corporate jargon and advertising slogans invading every surface of life
- Sudden tonal shifts from black comedy to existential dread
- Empathy as the last anchor — characters clinging to human connection as everything else dissolves
