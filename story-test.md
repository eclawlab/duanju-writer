Chapter 1: The Mysterious Visitor and the Compressed Biscuits
The Struggling Supermarket: The protagonist has operated the town’s only supermarket for three years without a single customer
. Due to rumors of bad "feng shui" and a lack of business, she prepares to close the shop for good
.
A Strange Arrival: Just before closing, a handsome man in ancient costume, Luo Changfeng, enters the shop
. Initially terrified that he might be a ghost, the protagonist realizes he is looking for "top-quality food"
.
The First Trade: She offers him military compressed biscuits, explaining that one piece can replace a full meal
. After demonstrating how to open the packaging and a bottle of water, Luo Changfeng enjoys the taste and decides to buy two boxes
.
A Supernatural Exit: He pays with a silver ingot, which the shopkeeper hesitantly accepts
. When she chases after him to check on the payment, he vanishes instantly after walking into a dead-end alley
.
Chapter 2: A Lucrative Discovery and the General’s Dilemma
The Pawn Shop Appraisal: The shopkeeper takes the silver ingot to a pawn shop and is shocked to find it is genuine; the owner offers her 7,000 yuan for it
. Realizing the massive profit margin—since the biscuits only cost her 250 yuan—she immediately orders 20 more boxes of biscuits and water
.
Luo Changfeng’s World: The story shifts to Luo Changfeng’s perspective in his own world, a city on the edge of a desert
. He is a general whose army is trapped outside Yongzhou, facing severe shortages of food and water
.
Testing the "Godly" Food: An army doctor tests the compressed biscuits for poison and finds them safe and delicious
. Luo Changfeng orders his men to collect all their silver and copper coins to buy more of this "mysterious" food
.
The Invisible Portal: Luo leads thirty armed soldiers to the alleyway where he found the shop, but they see only ruins
. It is revealed that only Luo Changfeng has the ability to see and enter the "Universal Grocery Store"
.
Chapter 3: Expanding the Trade and Ancient Mysteries
The Second Transaction: Luo Changfeng returns to the shop alone, bringing a large amount of silver and copper coins
. The shopkeeper gives him the 20 boxes of biscuits and water, along with "bonus" items: instant noodles, canned lunch meat, and a bottle of Erguotou liquor
.
The Worth of Ancient Currency: The shopkeeper returns to the pawn shop with two large ten-tael silver ingots, which she sells for 10,000 yuan each
.
The Mystery of the Coins: She also presents three copper coins
. The pawn shop owner is baffled because he has never seen the inscriptions before
. Despite their high quality and luster, he cannot verify their origin and takes photos to send to his mentor, a doctoral supervisor in cultural relics
.
Profitable Planning: The shopkeeper calculates her earnings, realizing she has made over 20,000 yuan in a single day, excluding the remaining broken silver and coins
. She decides to stock up even further to support her "wealthy" visitor
.
